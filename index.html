    Suggested single swaps

    A swap exchanges the date/time slot between two games — matchups don't change, only when each is played. Top 10 improvements shown.
    About / how this works

    Loads the schedule from DaySmart Recreation's public JSON:API (no login, no scraping). Stores in your browser's localStorage. Detects four issue classes and lets you simulate single-swap fixes that improve fairness.

    Issue weights (penalty function): game-count variance ×4, home/away imbalance ×1, slot-distribution variance ×1, Monday-share variance ×3 (Mondays are treated as less-desirable than other nights), games-per-team-per-week deviation from 1 ×3 (penalizes 2+ games in a week), head-to-head matchup variance ×3, same-matchup back-to-back ×8. Swaps that put a team in two games on the same calendar day are rejected outright.

    Caveats: A date-swap can't fix uneven game counts, home/away imbalance, or head-to-head matchup counts — those depend on the matchup list itself, not when games are scheduled. The swap engine still suggests fixes for slot fairness and back-to-back issues.
